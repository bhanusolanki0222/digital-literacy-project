# Case Study - UPI/Online Payment Fraud

## Crime Type: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

---

## Overview

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the fastest-growing cybercrimes in India. It exploits the convenience and widespread adoption of digital payment platforms such as PhonePe, Google Pay, and Paytm to deceive victims into transferring money to fraudsters.

---

## How It Typically Happens - Step by Step

**Scenario: The "Wrong Transfer" Scam targeting a college student**

1. **Contact:** A fraudster contacts the victim (a college student) via phone or WhatsApp, claiming to have accidentally transferred money to the victim's UPI account.

2. **False Proof:** The scammer sends a fabricated screenshot of a payment "confirmation" showing a large amount supposedly transferred to the victim's account.

3. **Urgency & Pressure:** The fraudster creates panic - "My family needs this money urgently, please send it back immediately."

4. **The Trap:** The victim, seeing no credit in their account, is asked to "confirm" the refund by scanning a QR code or entering their UPI PIN on a payment request sent by the scammer.

5. **Money Lost:** The moment the victim enters their PIN, the money is debited from *their own* account - not refunded. The fraudster disappears.

6. **No Recourse:** The money is often immediately transferred through multiple accounts, making recovery extremely difficult.

---

## Who is Targeted

- College students new to digital payments
- People selling items on OLX/Facebook Marketplace
- Small business owners and shopkeepers
- Elderly individuals unfamiliar with UPI mechanics

---

## Consequences

Victims lose money instantly, with limited chance of recovery. Beyond financial loss, victims often experience emotional distress, embarrassment, and a lasting distrust of digital payment systems. In some cases, the fraudster uses the victim's account details for further fraud, putting the victim at risk of legal complications.

**Source:** Based on reported patterns from the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) and NPCI fraud awareness guidelines.
