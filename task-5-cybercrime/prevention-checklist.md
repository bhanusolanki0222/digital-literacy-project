# Stay Safe Online - Prevention Checklist

## For College Students in India

---

| # | Safety Tip | Category |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or CVV with anyone** - no bank, app, or "support agent" will ever ask for these. | 💰 UPI Safety |
| 2 | **Receiving money does NOT require entering your PIN** - if someone asks you to enter your PIN to "receive" a refund or payment, it is a scam. Decline immediately. | 💰 UPI Safety |
| 3 | **Verify payment requests before scanning QR codes** - always check the recipient name on the payment app screen before confirming any transaction. | General Safety |
| 4 | **Use strong, unique passwords for all accounts** - avoid using your name, date of birth, or college roll number as passwords. Use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. | Account Security |
| 5 | **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)** on your email, social media, and banking apps for an extra layer of protection. | Account Security |
| 6 | **Do not click on links in SMS or WhatsApp messages** from unknown numbers, especially those claiming to be from your bank, NPCI, or government offices. | Phishing Prevention |
| 7 | **Download apps only from official sources** - use Google Play Store or Apple App Store. Avoid installing apps shared via links on WhatsApp or Telegram. | Device Safety |
| 8 | **Report cyber fraud immediately** - if you are scammed, call **Helpline 1930** (National Cyber Crime Helpline, available 24x7) or file a complaint at **cybercrime.gov.in** within the first few hours for the best chance of recovery. | Reporting |
