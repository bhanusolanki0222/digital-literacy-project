 Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students


 DO's


| 1 | Keep your profiles professional - Use a clear profile photo and a bio that reflects your academic identity. Employers and internship coordinators often look at social media. |
| 2 | Think before you post - Ask yourself: Would I be comfortable if my professor or future employer saw this? If not, don't post it. |
| 3 | Use privacy settings - Regularly review and update the privacy settings on all your social media accounts to control who sees your content. |
| 4 | Give credit when sharing content - If you share someone else's work, article, or image, always credit the original creator or source. |
| 5 | Report cyberbullying and harassment - Use platform tools to report abusive content or accounts. Do not engage with or retaliate against online harassers. |


---


 DON'Ts


| 1 | Don't share personal information publicly - Avoid posting your phone number, home address, college timetable, or travel plans publicly. |
| 2 | Don't engage in or encourage cyberbullying - Avoid sharing, liking, or commenting on content that mocks, threatens, or humiliates another person. |
| 3 | Don't spread misinformation - Always verify news or information before sharing it. Sharing fake news can damage your reputation and mislead others. |
| 4 | Don't use social media for venting about college or professors - Public complaints about your institution or faculty can have academic and professional consequences. |
| 5 | Don't accept friend/follow requests from unknown people - Especially from accounts with no mutual connections, no profile photo, or suspicious activity patterns. |
